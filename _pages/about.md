I am a PhD student at the [Laboratory for Physical Chemistry](https://chab.ethz.ch/en/research/institutes/LPC.html) at [ETH Zurich](https://www.ethz.ch/en.html). I work in the research group of [Prof. Markus Reiher](http://reiher.ethz.ch/). I develop procedures to apply methods from the field of machine learning and artificial intelligence to theoretical chemistry. My [first paper](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jctc.9b00627) applies Gaussian process regression and Bayesian sampling to dispersion interaction between organic compounds to yield more accurate results as well as streamline the pipeline of starting new reference calculations for compounds too dissimilar to the training set. 

In Fall 2018, I completed my Master's Thesis, _Enumerating the inorganic universe of small complexes for multifidelity machine learning_, at [MIT](http://web.mit.edu/) under [Prof. Heather J. Kulik](https://hjkgrp.mit.edu/). The work was [published](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlelanding/2020/me/c9me00069k#!divAbstract) in Molecular Systems Design & Engineering and I was given the opportunity to give a poster at the National Meeting 2018 of the ACS in Boston.

From 2013 to 2018 I studied [Interdisciplinary Science](https://chab.ethz.ch/en/studies/master/msc-interdisciplinary-sciences.html) at ETH Zurich with a major in Computational and Physical Chemistry. This research focused course allowed for less traditional intersections of the sciences: I attended classes in Machine Learning, Algorithms and Data Structures, and Deep Learning on one hand but also in Quantum Information Processing, Quantum Chemistry, and Spectroscopy on the other. This lets me combine my interests for the fundamental chemical sciences with modern information processing and statistical inference methods.
